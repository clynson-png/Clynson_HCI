
HCI HAPPY ENGINE
CROSS-ANALYSIS SPEC V1

PURPOSE

The HAPPY Engine must not rely on direct questionnaire scores alone.

The strongest insights emerge when subjective athlete perception is crossed with objective HCI performance data.

--------------------------------------------------
LEVEL V1 — DIRECT READING
--------------------------------------------------

Uses only athlete responses.

Examples:

CONFIDENCE
NERVE
HEED
ALIGNMENT
PLAN
POWERING
YEARN

Limitation:
Good for screening.
Weak for diagnosis.

--------------------------------------------------
LEVEL V2 — INTERNAL HAPPY CROSSING
--------------------------------------------------

Cross HAPPY and COMANDO variables.

Examples:

CONFIDENCE LOW + POWERING HIGH
= Athlete is working hard but lacks belief.

NERVE LOW + HEED LOW
= Athlete struggles with pressure and is unaware of arousal regulation.

PLAN LOW + ALIGNMENT LOW
= Coach-athlete direction problem.

YEARN HIGH + ORGANIZATION LOW
= High ambition with poor execution structure.

Outcome:
Explains WHY a score is low.

--------------------------------------------------
LEVEL V3 — HAPPY + HCI PERFORMANCE
--------------------------------------------------

Cross subjective data with objective HCI engines.

HAPPY
+ TARGETS
+ STRUCTURE
+ RHYTHM
+ RESULTS

Main Crossings:

CONFIDENCE × RESULT
Confidence supported by evidence?

CONFIDENCE × CONSISTENCY
Can confidence survive repetition?

NERVE × PRESSURE
Does perceived pressure match performance degradation?

HEED × RHYTHM
Does arousal affect rhythm?

HEED × EMOTIONAL
Does the athlete perceive emotional instability?

PLAN × PROCESS
Is the coach's plan becoming execution?

ALIGNMENT × TRANSFER
Does the athlete understand and transfer training?

POWERING × PHYSICAL
Does perceived energy match physical performance?

YEARN × TRAINING FREQUENCY
Does ambition match commitment?

ORGANIZATION × SESSION VARIABILITY
Does poor organization create inconsistent performance?

ANALYSIS × DEEPENING
Is the athlete learning or simply repeating?

OPPORTUNITY × TRAINING UTILIZATION
Is the athlete using the opportunities created by the coach?

--------------------------------------------------
PERCEPTION GAP MODEL
--------------------------------------------------

The most valuable insight is the gap between:

ATHLETE PERCEPTION
versus
OBJECTIVE EVIDENCE

Example:

CONFIDENCE = 5

CONSISTENCY = 2.4
PRESSURE = 2.1

Interpretation:

The athlete reports high confidence but objective evidence does not support it.
